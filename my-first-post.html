The Comfort of Experience

My startup journey.

November 2012, Startup Weekend Groningen. In a beautiful castle right in the city centre of Groningen my journey started. For 12 months I traveled through the hyped-up landscape of startups, just to end up in the same place...

From accelerator programs in Amsterdam and Dallas, pitch events in Eindhoven, San Francisco and everything in between, from The Next Web conference to TechCrunch Disrupt I've seen it all. A journey that included, paddle boarding in the Pacific Ocean, a hackathon on a bus, awesome waterslides in Tenerife and a lot more. I walked acros the Facebook campus, rode a Google bike, and fell asleep at the Soundcloud office in Berlin. All these events had one word in common, startup.

I've seen all kinds of nerds, geeks, designers, hipsters, entrepreneurs, marketeers, coders, geniuses, investors and bloggers. I've been impressed by all of them. I think there has been a point that I wanted to be each and every one of them.

Along the way I've felt discurraged by little 15 year old entrepreneurs. I've been hyped up by inspiring talks, scared by mean looking investors and comforted by unexpected mentors. Until I found myself back in that same castle in the city centre of Groningen. The same kid, just one tini tiny year older...

"Running a startup is a wild journey", that is something you hear everywhere. We even gave it a name, startuplife. Like it's a lifestyle. Now I won't disagree with this, I probably said myself a couple of times.

Recently, I started my first real startup job at Gibbon. A startup from Leiden, The Netherlands.

After my wild year living the startup life, traveling, networking, drinking beer, listing to pitches and drinking some more beer. I finally entered the real startup life. One where I actually have responsibilities, one where I have to make decisions, one where everything is for real.

I have been working at Gibbon for about two months I can say one thing. It's scary as hell!! All the experience I thought I had now seems worthless, all the things I thought I knew, now just seems a fraction of everything I need to know.

Standing on the sideline and asking questions to the people who are actually doing it is easy, but to go out there and answer those questions.. that is a whole different story. Although I sometimes feel a bit scared, or unsure which road to take I never feel discuraged.

The guys I work with. My team, has one thing that always comforts me. That is experience. Wouter, Joery and Petar have been working together for about five years. They set up a succesful web-agency together, and have been building software together for years. It's safe to say they know their shit.

Working with people that know their shit brings a lot of piece to mind. At least to me. Whenever something goes wrong, they fix it with ease. Maybe they are stressed as well on the inside, but all I get hear is a nerdy joke about how we are going to buy Facebook next week, and a big smile.

This experience showed me two things:

1. Team is everything. (yes, I heard over and over again at every startup event, but now I know why) This makes me extra proud to be on the Gibbon team.

2. Everybody will tell you something you should do. Don't go crazy over this, take on the advise that feels right, and don't forget how much you actually know.

I'm looking forward to my time ahead at Gibbon, and hopefully we'll be around for a while.

A big thank you to everybody who I got the meet over the last year. I learned from every single one of you, hopefully I'll be able to help others in the future as well.
